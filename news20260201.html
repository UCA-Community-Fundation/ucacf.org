Community in Action/社区在行动

This February, UCACF continued its mission of supporting grassroots organizations and community-led initiatives that strengthen Chinese American and broader Asian American communities. From cultural celebrations to civic engagement and mental health dialogue, these projects reflect the depth and diversity of work happening across our network.

2026年2月，UCA社区基金会（UCACF）持续支持扎根社区、由社区主导的公益项目，致力于强化华裔美国人及更广泛亚裔美国人社区的力量。从文化庆典到公民参与，从心理健康对话到社区关怀，这些项目充分展现了我们社区网络的深度与多样性。

Spotlight: ROSEMEAD — Film, Dialogue, and Community Care/项目聚焦：《ROSEMEAD》——影像、对话与社区关怀

One of the most meaningful moments in Jan was UCACF’s support of ROSEMEAD, a film that brings Asian family dynamics and mental health into open conversation.

Through the ROSEMEAD Buyout with United Chinese Americans (UCA-MA), UCACF helped create space for a community-centered screening and dialogue—demonstrating how storytelling can foster understanding, empathy, and connection.

This event reaffirmed our belief that community care is not only about services, but also about creating safe spaces where difficult conversations can happen collectively.

1月最具意义的项目之一，是 UCACF对电影《ROSEMEAD》的支持。这部影片将亚裔家庭关系与心理健康议题带入公开而真诚的讨论之中。

通过与美国华人联合会麻州分会（UCA-MA）合作的《ROSEMEAD》电影包场活动，UCACF 为社区打造了一个以对话为核心的观影与交流空间，展现了影像叙事如何促进理解、共情与连接。

这一活动再次印证了我们的理念：社区关怀不仅是服务的提供，更是为重要而复杂的议题创造安全、共同讨论的空间。

Looking Back: UCACF’s 2025 Impact/回顾：UCACF 的 2025 年影响力

As we begin a new year, we reflect on what UCACF accomplished in 2025:

81 grant applications received 57 projects approved (70.4% approval rate) $119,500 awarded in total grants Projects spanning multiple U.S. states, supporting both large metropolitan areas and smaller local communities

These grants supported cultural preservation, youth development, community care, and civic engagement—often providing the critical seed funding that allows grassroots organizations to move from idea to impact.

在新的一年开始之际，我们也回顾 UCACF 在 2025 年取得的成果：

共收到 81 份 资助申请
批准 57 个 项目（70.4% 通过率）
累计发放 119,500 美元 社区资助
项目覆盖 多个美国州，既支持大型城市社区，也服务于中小型本地社区

这些资助项目涵盖文化传承、青年发展、社区关怀与公民参与，往往成为组织从构想到落地的关键启动资金。
